a year ago
I had pretty high expectations going in, especially since one of my Korean friends spoke very highly of the place. We ordered the bossam ($50) and the sundubu-jjigae ($17), along with extra lettuce. The bossam was outstanding—the thickness of the pork was just right. I personally would have preferred a bit more herbal flavor, but it was still delicious. The inclusion of blanched or steamed onions and chives was a bit unconventional, but surprisingly good. The bossam-specific banchan were excellent, though the rest of the side dishes were fairly standard and not particularly memorable.

The sundubu-jjigae was decent—I’d probably go for a spicier version next time. Overall, the food quality was great, though the prices felt higher than expected. If you’re planning to visit, make a reservation if you can; otherwise, expect a wait. That said, arriving around 4:45 PM on a Saturday might get you seated within 5–10 minutes.

Given the price point, I don’t see myself returning soon, but it’s definitely a solid spot for anyone new to Korean cuisine—at least for the two dishes we tried.

If you're planning a visit to Modoo, definitely keep in mind that the portions are generous! If you're going with a smaller group, you might not need to order too many dishes—though with everything tasting so delicious, it's tempting to try it all. The service is impressively quick; we got our food in no time after ordering. Just a heads-up: they don't take reservations, so if you’re going on a busy evening like Friday around 7pm, expect a bit of a wait for a table. The crowd tends to lean towards teenagers and families, which gives it a lively atmosphere. Overall, a great spot, but pace yourself with the food orders!
