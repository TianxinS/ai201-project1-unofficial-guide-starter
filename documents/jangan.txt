If you are looking for the culinary equivalent of a warm hug, look no further than the Brisket Sullungtang at JangAn. This traditional Korean ox-bone soup is a masterclass in balance, depth, and soul-satisfying comfort.
From the moment the stone pot arrives at the table, bubbling and steaming, you know you’re in for a treat. The broth is the star of the show—milky white, rich, and velvety. It’s clear that this broth has been simmered for hours, extracting every bit of flavor from the bones without ever feeling heavy or greasy. It’s clean, pure, and serves as the perfect canvas for the generous portions of thinly sliced brisket.
The Highlights:
The Brisket: The meat is fresh and of excellent quality. It’s lean yet flavorful, absorbing the essence of the soup.
The Texture: The addition of silky somyeon (thin wheat noodles) provides a wonderful textural contrast, making every bite interesting.
The Banchan: No Sullungtang is complete without its sidekicks. The kimchi and kkakdugi (radish kimchi) at JangAn are vibrant, crisp, and offer the perfect acidic punch to cut through the richness of the ox bone broth.
The experience at JangAn feels authentic and unpretentious. The soup arrives unseasoned, allowing you to customize it with sea salt and a mountain of fresh green onions to your exact preference. It’s a dish that feels both healthy and indulgent at the same time.
